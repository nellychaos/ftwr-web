# Code of Conduct

FTWR is a community commons. This code describes the behaviour expected from everyone who participates -- contributors, stewards, and readers -- across The Map, the Forum, the Archive, the RFC process, and every other surface the commons operates.

Short version: be honest, attribute credit, argue about ideas rather than people, and assume the other person is a thoughtful modeller having a bad day.

## Expected Behaviour

- **Argue about the work, not the person.** Disagreement is welcome and useful. Personal attacks, character judgments, and ad hominem arguments are not.
- **Credit where credit is due.** When you build on someone else's contribution, name them. When you disagree with a published view, quote it accurately before responding.
- **Assume good faith first.** A poorly-phrased question from a newcomer is not bait. A missed reference in a PR is rarely malicious. Ask for clarification before assuming the worst.
- **Show your work.** If you claim a method is wrong, show the evidence. If you claim an entry is miscategorised, cite the criteria. Assertions without reasoning are not arguments.
- **Make room for newcomers.** Jargon, acronyms, and inside references make the commons smaller. Define terms on first use where it costs you nothing.
- **Disclose material interests.** If you have a financial stake in a Map entry, an RFC outcome, or a guest-post subject, say so up front. See `GOVERNANCE.md` § Conflict of Interest.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or sustained personal attacks
- Discriminatory language or jokes, including about race, gender, sexuality, religion, disability, or national origin
- Doxxing, deanonymisation, or attempts to link a contributor's handle to their real identity
- Sexual content or unwelcome advances in any commons surface
- Spam, including unsolicited promotional content, affiliate links, or repeated link-drops
- Bad-faith submissions: tools that do not match their description, RFCs that rehash settled policy, forum posts designed to provoke rather than discuss
- Coordinated manipulation: brigading threads, voting-ring behaviour on the forum, coordinated PR flooding
- Threats of any kind, including legal threats used to suppress discussion of a tool, method, or entry

## Specific Guidance

### On the Forum

- Threads are public and indexed. Post as if you are writing for someone finding the discussion in a search engine two years from now.
- Quote the specific claim you are responding to. Long threads drift without anchors.
- If a thread is heating up, step away for an hour before replying. The commons rewards thoughtful responses, not fast ones.

### In Map PRs

- Neutral descriptions only. Any adjective doing promotional work gets edited out in review.
- Cite your conflict of interest if you maintain, work for, or are paid by the tool.
- Do not re-submit a rejected entry without addressing the rejection reason.

### In RFC Discussion

- Distinguish "I disagree with this policy" from "this policy is wrong for these specific reasons with this specific evidence." Only the second is a useful RFC comment.
- Silence is not consent. If an RFC is moving toward acceptance and you have unaddressed concerns, post them. Stewards will not assume agreement from absence.

### In the Archive

- Guest posts must be the author's own work. Quoting and citation welcome; unattributed paraphrase is plagiarism and grounds for removal.
- Claims of market inefficiency, model performance, or edge require evidence (code, data, references). Undocumented claims are edited or removed.

## Enforcement

Stewards are the initial point of enforcement. Enforcement actions, in order of severity:

1. **Private clarification.** A steward reaches out via forum DM to clarify expectations. Most incidents stop here.
2. **Public correction.** A steward responds in-thread noting the issue and pointing to this document.
3. **Post removal.** Posts that violate this code are edited or removed, with a note in-thread explaining why.
4. **Temporary suspension.** Repeated or serious violations result in a time-boxed ban from forum posting and PR submission (typically 7-30 days).
5. **Permanent ban.** For severe or repeated violations after prior suspension, the handle is permanently banned from all commons surfaces.

Decisions at level 4 and 5 require rough consensus of the active steward pool, documented in a Meta forum thread. The affected contributor may appeal via an RFC proposing a different outcome.

## Reporting

To report a violation, use one of:

- Post in the **Meta** category on the forum (appropriate when the incident is already public and needs a visible steward response)
- Open a private forum DM to any steward (appropriate for sensitive reports)
- Email the fiscal host directly if the report concerns a steward themselves (appropriate when going through stewards is inappropriate)

Reports will be acknowledged within 7 days and resolved within 30 days. Serious reports may be resolved faster or escalated to the fiscal host.

The reporter's identity is protected unless they explicitly consent to disclosure. Retaliation against reporters is itself a violation of this code.

## Steward Conduct

Stewards are held to a higher standard than contributors. Actions expected of stewards:

- Model the behaviour described here. A steward who violates the code they enforce loses legitimacy.
- Recuse from enforcement decisions involving contributors with whom they have a personal dispute, a financial relationship, or any other material conflict.
- Accept public criticism of moderation calls without retaliation. Disagreement with a steward decision is not itself a code violation.

Violations by stewards follow the same escalation process, with one addition: the offending steward does not participate in the rough-consensus decision about their own conduct.

## Scope

This code applies to:

- The forum at `community.ftwr.app`
- All repositories under `github.com/ftwr-dev`
- The Archive at `blog.ftwr.app`, including comments if enabled
- The landing and Map surfaces at `ftwr.app`
- The Ledger and Open Collective at `opencollective.com/ftwr`
- Any official FTWR account on external platforms (X, Reddit, etc.)
- Any event, call, or gathering organised under the FTWR name

It does not cover contributors' behaviour on platforms and in contexts unrelated to FTWR. However, serious off-platform conduct that would constitute harassment of another FTWR contributor may be grounds for action under this code.
