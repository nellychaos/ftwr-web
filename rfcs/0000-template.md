# RFC-0000: Short imperative title

## Summary

One paragraph. What does this RFC propose, in plain language? A reader skimming the archive index should understand the gist from this paragraph alone.

## Motivation

State the problem. Why is this worth changing? What happens if the commons does nothing?

If there is prior discussion -- forum threads, related RFCs, external references -- link it here. Motivate the RFC from observed reality in the commons, not from hypothetical concerns.

## Proposal

State the proposed change. Be specific enough that a steward could implement it without further interpretation.

For policy changes: quote the exact text being added, removed, or modified, in context. For structural changes (categories, schema fields, workflows): describe the new state and the migration path.

## Rationale and Alternatives

Why this proposal, and not something else?

List the alternatives considered. For each, explain why it was rejected. "Do nothing" is always an alternative worth considering.

Name trade-offs honestly. Every policy costs something. Describe what this proposal costs and who pays the cost.

## Prior Art

Reference prior RFCs, papers, or policies from other commons that addressed similar questions. Python PEPs, Rust RFCs, IETF RFCs, and academic literature are all fair game.

If this proposal contradicts prior art, explain why.

## Unresolved Questions

List open questions this RFC does not answer. These may be resolved during discussion or deferred to future RFCs.

## Impact

Describe the expected impact on:

- Contributors: what changes about how they work?
- Stewards: what changes about moderation, review, or maintenance?
- The Map, Starters, Archive, or Forum: what operational changes follow?
- Existing entries or content: is there a migration? Grandfathering? Deprecation?

## Adoption Plan

If accepted, how is this RFC implemented? Name the concrete follow-up actions and who is expected to do them. An accepted RFC with no adoption plan is a wish list.

- **Decided by:** list of stewards who participated in the rough-consensus decision
- **Rationale:** short summary of why the RFC reached this status. Required for `rejected` and `withdrawn`.
